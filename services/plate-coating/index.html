Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay
The ELISA Process

Experienced ELISA specialists immobilize the antigen, antibody, or other compound on the well surface for the purpose of a binding assay.

Coating buffers stabilize the antigen or antibody—which is used to coat the ELISA multiwell plate—maximizing absorption to the plate and optimizing interactions with the detection antibody.

Simplicity, Selectivity & Sensitivity
The Advantages of ELISA Microplate Coating

Sensitivity

High specificity and sensitivity due to the antigen-antibody reaction.

Eco-Friendliness

Radioactive substances and large amounts of organic solvents are not required, making ELISA generally safe and eco-friendly.

Efficiency

Simultaneous analyses can be performed without complicated sample pre-treatment, providing a high level of efficiency.

Cost-Effectiveness

When compared to alternative methods, ELISA is a generally cost-effective assay.
